Alliance Contracting

Collaborative Project Delivery

Model Definition

Alliance Contracting is a **collaborative project delivery method** where the owner, contractors, and key stakeholders form an **alliance team** to jointly deliver a project. Risk and reward are shared, and decisions are made collectively to **optimize project outcomes**.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership remains with the project sponsor
  • All alliance members share decision-making authority
  • Joint accountability for cost, schedule, and quality

Strategic Applications

Large & Complex Projects

Alliance contracting is ideal for **highly complex or high-risk projects** where integration, collaboration, and innovation are critical for success.

Infrastructure & Energy Sectors

Frequently used in highways, rail, pipelines, power plants, and major industrial facilities where **shared objectives** drive project delivery.

Project Suitability Analysis

Alliance contracting is suitable for projects with **high uncertainty, multiple stakeholders, and need for innovative solutions**, ensuring all parties are aligned toward achieving the best outcomes.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks are **shared** among alliance members, including cost overruns, schedule delays, and quality issues. Mitigation relies on **joint governance, performance metrics, and transparent collaboration frameworks**.
