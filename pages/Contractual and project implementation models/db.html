Design–Build

DB – Engineering & Construction

Model Definition

Design–Build (DB) is a **project delivery method** where the contractor is responsible for both **design and construction**. By integrating design and execution under a single contract, coordination issues are minimized, and project delivery can be faster and more efficient.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership remains with the project sponsor
  • Single point of responsibility for design and construction
  • Reduced risk of design-execution conflicts

Strategic Applications

Fast-Track Projects

DB is ideal for projects with **tight schedules**, where integrating design and construction reduces delays and accelerates project delivery.

Infrastructure & Utilities

Roads, bridges, water/wastewater systems, and industrial facilities benefit from a single contractor managing both design and execution.

Project Suitability Analysis

Design–Build is suitable for projects requiring **streamlined communication**, reduced design-construction conflicts, and faster delivery schedules while maintaining high quality.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Key risks include design errors, coordination issues, and cost overruns. Mitigation relies on clear contract scopes, single-point responsibility, and milestone-based monitoring.
