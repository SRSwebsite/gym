EPCC

Engineering · Procurement · Construction & Commissioning

Model Definition

EPCC is a **comprehensive project execution model** where the contractor is responsible for **engineering, procurement, construction, and commissioning** of a facility. The contractor delivers a fully operational asset ready for use, ensuring all systems are tested, integrated, and functioning according to specifications.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership remains with the project sponsor or public entity
  • Contractor ensures asset is fully commissioned and operational
  • Risk of execution and commissioning managed contractually with performance guarantees

Strategic Applications

Complex Industrial Plants

EPCC is ideal for large-scale industrial and energy projects where commissioning and operational readiness are critical for safe and efficient start-up.

Infrastructure & Utilities

Roads, bridges, power plants, and water/wastewater facilities benefit from EPCC when sponsors require delivery of a fully operational, commissioned asset.

Project Suitability Analysis

EPCC is suitable for **highly complex projects** requiring tight integration of engineering, procurement, construction, and commissioning activities to deliver operational readiness at project handover.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Key risks include delays in commissioning, integration errors, and construction defects. Mitigation relies on detailed specifications, quality assurance processes, milestone testing, and performance guarantees.
