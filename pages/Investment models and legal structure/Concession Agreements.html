Concession Agreements

Contractual Project Structure

Definition

A Concession Agreement is a **formal contract between a public authority and a private entity**, granting the private party the right to operate and maintain a public asset or service for a specified period, under agreed conditions, after which ownership reverts to the public authority.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership usually remains with the government/public authority
  • Private operator has **operational control** under contract terms
  • Responsibilities, risk allocation, and revenue rights clearly defined

Strategic Applications

Transport Infrastructure

Highways, bridges, ports, and railways often use concession agreements to allow private investment while ensuring public ownership.

Utilities & Public Services

Water supply, wastewater treatment, and other essential services adopt concession structures to improve efficiency and service quality.

Project Suitability Analysis

Concession Agreements are suitable for projects requiring **private operation and financing, risk allocation, and defined service standards**, while maintaining **public ownership** of strategic assets.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **regulatory changes, demand fluctuation, and operator default**. Mitigation involves **contractual safeguards, government guarantees, and performance monitoring**.
