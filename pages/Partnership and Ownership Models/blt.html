BLT

Build · Lease · Transfer

Model Definition

BLT (Build, Lease, Transfer) is a financing and delivery model in which a private entity constructs an asset and leases it to a public or institutional client for a fixed period, after which ownership is transferred to the lessee.

Ownership & Control

  • Private ownership during lease term
  • Operational responsibility typically with lessee
  • Automatic transfer at lease completion

Strategic Applications

Government & Institutional Buildings

BLT is commonly used for ministries, municipal buildings, schools, and healthcare facilities where public entities prefer gradual payment through leasing.

Social & Civic Infrastructure

Housing, training centers, and public service complexes benefit from BLT structures due to predictable costs and simplified risk allocation.

Project Suitability Analysis

BLT models are best suited for non-revenue-generating assets with stable public demand. They reduce immediate fiscal pressure while ensuring asset ownership is transferred after lease completion.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks involve lessee payment risk, construction delays, and asset condition at transfer. Proper BLT agreements define maintenance duties, lease indexation, and handover standards clearly.
