BOT

Build · Operate · Transfer

Model Definition

BOT (Build, Operate, Transfer) is a public–private partnership model in which a private investor or consortium finances, constructs, and operates an infrastructure or industrial asset for a defined concession period, after which ownership is transferred to the public sector or project owner.

Ownership & Control

  • Temporary ownership during concession period
  • Operational control limited to agreed timeframe
  • Mandatory asset transfer at end of term

Strategic Applications

Transport & Infrastructure

BOT is extensively used in highways, railways, ports, airports, and logistics hubs where public ownership is essential but private efficiency is required during operations.

Utilities & Public Services

Water treatment plants, power transmission assets, district heating, and waste management facilities commonly adopt BOT structures in emerging Eurasian markets.

Project Suitability Analysis

BOT models are best suited for capital-intensive projects with clear public interest, predictable demand, and defined concession frameworks. They enable governments to leverage private capital and expertise while retaining long-term ownership of strategic assets.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks include concession renegotiation, demand shortfalls, tariff regulation, and political change. Risk mitigation typically relies on government guarantees, minimum revenue schemes, and balanced concession agreements.
