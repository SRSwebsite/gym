ROO

Rehabilitate · Own · Operate

Model Definition

ROO (Rehabilitate, Own, Operate) is a PPP model in which a private entity rehabilitates an existing asset, gains full ownership, and operates it for revenue generation without transfer to the public sector.

Ownership & Control

  • Private ownership post-rehabilitation
  • Full operational and revenue control
  • No mandatory handback to public sector

Strategic Applications

Power Plants & Utilities

ROO is applied to existing power stations, water facilities, or industrial assets where private ownership ensures revenue and efficiency incentives.

Transport & Concessioned Assets

Roads, bridges, and ports that are rehabilitated and operated privately for tolls or fees fit the ROO model perfectly.

Project Suitability Analysis

ROO is ideal for mature assets with revenue potential where governments prefer divestment. It aligns private incentives for rehabilitation and operational efficiency.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks include overestimating revenue potential, rehabilitation cost overruns, and regulatory changes. Mitigation relies on thorough due diligence, robust financing, and operational expertise.
