Blended Finance

Development & Private-Public Investment

Model Definition

Blended Finance combines **public funds, development aid, and private investment** to finance projects that are socially or environmentally impactful, but may carry high risk for private investors. This approach leverages **subsidies, guarantees, and concessional capital** to attract commercial investment.

Ownership & Control

  • Project ownership remains with the developer or SPV
  • Public and private investors share financial risk but usually not operational control
  • Governance includes reporting requirements for both development outcomes and financial performance

Strategic Applications

High-Risk Development Projects

Blended Finance attracts **private investment to projects with social/environmental objectives**, such as renewable energy, water access, and urban development.

Public-Private Partnerships with Development Goals

Combines **public guarantees and private capital** to reduce risk and enhance the financial viability of infrastructure and social impact projects.

Project Suitability Analysis

Suitable for projects that require **mobilizing private capital** while delivering **social, environmental, or development outcomes**, especially in emerging markets.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **project execution, credit risk, and coordination among stakeholders**. Mitigation involves **guarantees, blended instruments, and clear governance structures**.
