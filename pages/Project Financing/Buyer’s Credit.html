Buyer’s Credit

Bank-Financed Export Support

Model Definition

Buyer’s Credit is a financing method where a **bank or financial institution lends directly to the buyer** of goods or services, allowing the buyer to pay the supplier immediately while **repaying the loan over a deferred schedule**.

Ownership & Control

  • Project or equipment ownership transfers to buyer upon delivery
  • Bank provides funding but does not control operations
  • Buyer retains full operational and management authority

Strategic Applications

Capital-Intensive Equipment Projects

Buyer’s Credit enables buyers to **procure expensive equipment** from international suppliers without upfront capital constraints.

Cross-Border Export Projects

Facilitates **international contracts**, allowing exporters to receive payment promptly while buyers repay banks over agreed terms.

Project Suitability Analysis

Suitable for projects where **buyer liquidity is limited**, but suppliers need immediate payment, and bank financing can bridge the gap.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **buyer default, currency fluctuations, and bank credit exposure**. Mitigation involves **guarantees, insurance, and structured repayment plans**.
