Corporate Finance

Balance Sheet Financing

Model Definition

Corporate Finance, or Balance Sheet Financing, is a **financing approach where the company's own balance sheet is used** to fund projects. The company assumes full responsibility for debt repayment and financial risks, without isolating the project in a separate SPV.

Ownership & Control

  • Project assets are fully controlled by the parent company
  • Equity and debt remain on the company’s balance sheet
  • Company manages operational and financial risks directly

Strategic Applications

Internal Corporate Projects

Suitable for projects fully funded by the company without the need for separate project-specific financing.

Repeatable or Smaller-Scale Projects

Ideal for projects that are **less capital-intensive**, recurring, or integrated within the company’s operations.

Project Suitability Analysis

Balance Sheet Financing is suitable when the company can **absorb debt on its own balance sheet, has sufficient cash flow, and seeks flexibility** in funding and management.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **over-leverage, cash flow strain, and financial exposure**. Mitigation requires **strong corporate governance, financial planning, and risk monitoring**.
