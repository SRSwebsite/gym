Islamic Finance

Murabaha, Ijara, Sukuk

Model Definition

Islamic Finance provides **Shariah-compliant financing solutions** for projects and investments. Key instruments include: Murabaha (cost-plus sale), Ijara (lease-to-own), and Sukuk (Islamic bonds), enabling capital access without violating prohibition on interest (Riba).

Ownership & Control

  • Project ownership typically lies with the borrower or SPV
  • Financial institutions structure the transaction but do not control operations
  • Assets may be leased (Ijara) or financed via ownership transfer (Murabaha/Sukuk)

Strategic Applications

Energy and Infrastructure Projects

Islamic Finance is widely used for **power plants, highways, and water infrastructure**, ensuring compliance with Shariah principles.

Real Estate and Industrial Development

Enables developers and investors to access capital for **construction, industrial projects, and urban development**, using Murabaha, Ijara, or Sukuk.

Project Suitability Analysis

Suitable for projects requiring **Shariah-compliant financing**, investor participation, and risk-sharing structures, while avoiding interest-based loans.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **structuring complexity, compliance verification, and liquidity management**. Mitigation involves using **expert Shariah boards, clear contracts, and risk-sharing arrangements**.
