Mezzanine Finance

Hybrid Debt-Equity Financing

Model Definition

Mezzanine Finance is a **hybrid financing instrument** positioned between senior debt and equity. It often includes **subordinated debt with equity warrants or profit participation**, offering higher returns than conventional loans while providing partial ownership upside.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership remains with existing shareholders, but mezzanine investors may have **conversion rights or warrants**
  • Mezzanine lenders typically do not control daily operations
  • Governance rights may include oversight on key financial decisions

Strategic Applications

Corporate Expansion

Used to finance growth initiatives, acquisitions, or recapitalization of companies that need **capital beyond senior debt capacity**.

Project Financing with High Growth Potential

Provides funding for projects where **equity alone is insufficient**, but investors seek **higher returns than senior debt**.

Project Suitability Analysis

Suitable for **companies or projects with stable cash flow and growth potential**, where mezzanine financing can bridge the gap between senior debt and equity.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **subordination to senior debt, liquidity constraints, and conversion timing**. Mitigation involves **clear covenants, profit-sharing terms, and due diligence on cash flow stability**.
