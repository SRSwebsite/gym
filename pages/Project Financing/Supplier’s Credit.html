Supplier’s Credit

Vendor Financing

Model Definition

Supplier’s Credit is a financing method where the **equipment or service supplier provides credit** to the buyer. The buyer can defer payment over a specified period, reducing the need for immediate capital and facilitating project execution.

Ownership & Control

  • Project or equipment ownership usually transfers upon delivery or partial payment
  • Supplier retains financial claim until full repayment
  • Operational control remains with the buyer

Strategic Applications

Equipment-Intensive Projects

Supplier’s Credit is ideal for projects requiring **expensive equipment or services**, allowing buyers to defer payments while executing the project.

International Vendor Projects

Facilitates **cross-border projects** where exporters provide financing support to buyers to ensure contract execution.

Project Suitability Analysis

Suitable for projects where **supplier financing reduces upfront capital requirements**, mitigates immediate liquidity constraints, and accelerates project start-up.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Key risks include **supplier default, delayed payments, or buyer credit risk**. Mitigation may involve **insurance, guarantees, or bank support**.
