FIDIC Contracts

Red, Yellow & Silver Books

Model Definition

FIDIC contracts are **standardized international contracts** widely used in infrastructure, industrial, and large-scale projects. Each “book” serves a different purpose:

  • Red Book: Owner designs, contractor builds
  • Yellow Book: Contractor designs and builds (Design & Build)
  • Silver Book: EPC / Turnkey delivery, contractor assumes most risk

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership of the project remains with the owner
  • Contractor responsibilities vary by book type
  • Clear risk allocation and performance obligations

Strategic Applications

Owner-Designed Projects

Red Book is ideal for projects where the owner provides complete design, and the contractor focuses solely on construction.

Design & Build / EPC

Yellow and Silver Books are suitable when contractors handle design, procurement, and construction, transferring more risk to the contractor.

Project Suitability Analysis

FIDIC contracts are suitable for projects requiring **international standards, clear risk allocation, and structured dispute resolution**, ensuring predictability and enforceability across jurisdictions.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks depend on the chosen FIDIC book: Red Book transfers design responsibility to the owner, while Silver Book places significant risk on the contractor. **Proper contract selection and legal expertise** are critical to manage cost, schedule, and performance risks.
