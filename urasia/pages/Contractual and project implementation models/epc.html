EPC

Engineering · Procurement · Construction

Model Definition

EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) is a **turnkey project execution model** where a contractor is responsible for delivering a fully functional asset, handling engineering, procurement, and construction phases. Ownership remains with the project sponsor, while the EPC contractor focuses on delivering on time, on budget, and per specifications.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership and operation remain with project sponsor/public entity
  • Contractor is fully accountable for design, procurement, and construction
  • No post-completion operational responsibility

Strategic Applications

Power Plants & Industrial Facilities

EPC contracts are widely applied in constructing complex industrial and power facilities where design, procurement, and construction phases must be tightly coordinated for turnkey delivery.

Infrastructure & Utilities

Roads, bridges, pipelines, and water treatment facilities often use EPC to ensure projects are delivered on schedule, within budget, and meeting quality standards.

Project Suitability Analysis

EPC is ideal for **greenfield projects** or technically complex projects with well-defined scope. The model transfers design and construction risk to the contractor while maintaining sponsor ownership.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Key risks include design errors, procurement delays, cost overruns, and schedule slippage. Mitigation relies on detailed specifications, fixed-price contracts, milestone-based payments, and performance guarantees.
