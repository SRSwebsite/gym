EPCM

Engineering · Procurement · Construction Management

Model Definition

EPCM is a **project management-focused execution model** where the contractor manages engineering, procurement, and construction activities on behalf of the owner. Unlike EPC, the EPCM contractor **does not deliver a turnkey asset**, but ensures the project is executed according to schedule, budget, and quality standards.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership remains fully with project sponsor
  • Contractor coordinates contractors, suppliers, and vendors
  • Risk of cost overruns and completion lies primarily with the owner

Strategic Applications

Complex Industrial Projects

EPCM is ideal for projects where multiple contractors and suppliers are involved and the owner requires a dedicated manager to coordinate engineering, procurement, and construction activities efficiently.

Infrastructure & Utilities

Roads, bridges, power plants, and water treatment facilities benefit from EPCM when the owner seeks active oversight and wants to maintain control over design choices and cost decisions.

Project Suitability Analysis

EPCM is suitable for technically complex projects where the owner wants **high involvement** in execution decisions, cost control, and quality management while leveraging contractor expertise for coordination.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Key risks include cost overruns, schedule delays, and coordination failures. Mitigation relies on structured management contracts, clear scopes, regular reporting, and performance monitoring.
