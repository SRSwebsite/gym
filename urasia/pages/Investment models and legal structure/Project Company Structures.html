Project Company Structures

ProjectCo & Governance

Model Definition

Project Company Structures involve the creation of a **dedicated Project Company (ProjectCo)** to execute, finance, and manage a specific project. This structure centralizes ownership, governance, and financial flows.

Ownership & Control

  • Owned by investors, lenders, and sometimes contractors
  • Governed by a formal board and shareholder agreements
  • Defines rights, responsibilities, and cash flow allocation clearly

Strategic Applications

Centralized Project Management

ProjectCo structures centralize **ownership, contracts, and cash flow** to streamline project execution and financial management.

Multi-Investor / Multi-Partner Projects

Enables collaboration among **equity investors, lenders, and contractors** while clearly defining rights and responsibilities.

Project Suitability Analysis

Project Company Structures are suitable for **large, complex projects** requiring **clear governance, aligned stakeholder interests, and dedicated financial management**.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **misalignment among stakeholders, financial mismanagement, and operational inefficiencies**. Mitigation involves **formal governance, contracts, and reporting mechanisms**.
