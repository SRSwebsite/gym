Joint Venture

Project Investment & Ownership

Model Definition

A Joint Venture (JV) is a **strategic partnership between two or more parties** to undertake a specific project or business activity. Partners share **capital, expertise, resources, risks, and profits** according to their agreement.

Ownership & Control

  • Equity and voting rights shared among partners
  • Decision-making aligned with JV agreement
  • Can be structured as a separate legal entity (SPV) or contractual JV

Strategic Applications

Cross-Company Collaboration

Ideal for projects where partners contribute **complementary skills, resources, or technology** to achieve strategic goals.

Large-Scale & International Projects

Commonly used in **international infrastructure, energy, or industrial projects**, allowing shared capital investment and risk.

Project Suitability Analysis

JV is suitable for projects requiring **combined expertise, resource sharing, and strategic partnerships**, where collaboration maximizes operational efficiency and profitability.

Risk Considerations & Mitigation

Risks include **conflict of interest, misaligned objectives, and operational disputes**. Mitigation relies on **detailed JV agreements, governance frameworks, and defined exit strategies**.
