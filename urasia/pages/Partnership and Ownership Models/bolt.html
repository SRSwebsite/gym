BOLT

Build · Operate · Lease · Transfer

Model Definition

BOLT (Build, Operate, Lease, Transfer) is a project delivery and financing model in which a private entity builds and operates an asset, leases it to a public or institutional user for a fixed period, and transfers ownership at the end of the lease term.

Ownership & Control

  • Private ownership during lease period
  • Operational responsibility retained by developer
  • Asset transferred upon lease expiration

Strategic Applications

Public Buildings & Facilities

BOLT is frequently used for schools, hospitals, administrative buildings, and judicial facilities where governments prefer leasing rather than direct capital expenditure.

Commercial & Mixed-Use Assets

Office complexes, logistics centers, and institutional housing projects benefit from BOLT structures due to predictable lease income and defined exit conditions.

Project Suitability Analysis

BOLT models are ideal for assets with stable occupancy and creditworthy lessees. They shift upfront construction costs to the private sector while enabling public entities to access facilities through long-term lease arrangements.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Primary risks include lessee credit risk, lease renegotiation, maintenance obligations, and asset handover condition. Well-structured BOLT contracts clearly allocate maintenance, insurance, and performance responsibilities.
