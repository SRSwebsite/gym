BOO

Build · Own · Operate

Model Definition

BOO (Build, Own, Operate) is a long-term ownership-based project delivery and investment model in which the private investor or consortium assumes full responsibility for financing, designing, constructing, owning, and operating an asset throughout its economic life, without any contractual obligation to transfer ownership to the public sector.

Ownership & Control

  • Permanent ownership retained by investor or SPV
  • Operational autonomy over pricing and utilization
  • Regulatory oversight without asset reversion

Strategic Applications

Energy & Utilities

BOO is widely applied in independent power generation, renewable energy facilities, and utility-scale infrastructure where long-term demand stability and predictable offtake mechanisms exist.

Industrial & Processing Assets

Suitable for petrochemical complexes, refineries, processing plants, and heavy industrial facilities where continuous operation and asset optimization directly impact profitability.

Project Suitability Analysis

BOO models are most effective in projects with stable long-term demand, limited political interference, and clearly defined regulatory frameworks. They favor investors seeking asset-backed returns, operational influence, and long-term value creation rather than short-term exit strategies.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risk factors include regulatory changes, tariff controls, currency exposure, and market demand shifts. However, these risks are often offset by higher return potential, asset appreciation, and full strategic control over operations.
