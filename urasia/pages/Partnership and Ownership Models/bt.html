BT

Build · Transfer

Model Definition

BT (Build, Transfer) is a project delivery model in which a private contractor designs and constructs an asset and transfers ownership immediately upon completion to the public authority or project owner.

Ownership & Control

  • Immediate transfer upon completion
  • No operational or revenue rights
  • Public sector retains full lifecycle control

Strategic Applications

Core Infrastructure

Roads, bridges, public buildings, and utilities often adopt BT models where governments seek fast delivery without long-term private involvement.

Industrial & Municipal Projects

Treatment plants, substations, and civic facilities use BT structures when operational control must remain fully public.

Project Suitability Analysis

BT models are ideal for projects with secure public funding and limited revenue-generation potential. They minimize private-sector exposure while ensuring timely asset delivery.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks include construction delays, cost overruns, and quality compliance. These are typically managed through fixed-price contracts, performance bonds, and strict acceptance criteria.
