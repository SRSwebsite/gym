DBFM

Design · Build · Finance · Maintain

Model Definition

DBFM (Design, Build, Finance, Maintain) is a public–private partnership model where the private partner is responsible for designing, constructing, financing, and maintaining an asset, while operations typically remain with the public sector.

Ownership & Control

  • Public sector retains operational control
  • Private sector ensures asset availability
  • Maintenance obligations defined contractually

Strategic Applications

Hospitals & Healthcare Facilities

DBFM is commonly used in hospitals and medical campuses where clinical operations remain public while private partners maintain buildings and systems.

Educational & Civic Buildings

Schools, universities, courthouses, and administrative buildings benefit from DBFM structures that ensure long-term asset condition and predictable costs.

Project Suitability Analysis

DBFM models are ideal for assets where service delivery is sensitive and should remain public. They align lifecycle maintenance incentives without transferring operational control.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Main risks include long-term maintenance underperformance, lifecycle cost misestimation, and interface risk between operator and maintainer. These are mitigated through KPIs, availability deductions, and handback requirements.
