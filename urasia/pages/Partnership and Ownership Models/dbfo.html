DBFO

Design · Build · Finance · Operate

Model Definition

DBFO (Design, Build, Finance, Operate) is a public–private partnership model in which a private consortium is responsible for the design, construction, financing, and long-term operation of an asset, typically without asset transfer at contract end.

Ownership & Control

  • Private financing and lifecycle responsibility
  • Public ownership may remain throughout
  • Operation governed by performance standards

Strategic Applications

Transport Infrastructure

Roads, tunnels, bridges, and transit systems frequently use DBFO models to align construction quality with long-term operational performance.

Social Infrastructure

Hospitals, schools, prisons, and public buildings adopt DBFO structures where availability and service levels drive payment.

Project Suitability Analysis

DBFO models are optimal for projects with stable public funding and measurable service outputs. They incentivize whole-life cost optimization by integrating design, finance, and operations under one contract.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks include financing cost volatility, long-term performance failure, and contract rigidity. These are mitigated through availability payments, performance deductions, and refinancing mechanisms.
