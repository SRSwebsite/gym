DBOT

Design · Build · Operate · Transfer

Model Definition

DBOT (Design, Build, Operate, Transfer) is an integrated project delivery model in which a private entity is responsible for the design, construction, and operation of an asset for a defined concession period before transferring it to the public owner.

Ownership & Control

  • Private operational control during concession
  • Design and performance accountability
  • Mandatory transfer at end of term

Strategic Applications

Smart Infrastructure & Transport

DBOT is well suited for metro systems, intelligent transport networks, and large-scale infrastructure requiring seamless design-to-operation integration.

Utilities & Environmental Projects

Water treatment plants, desalination facilities, and waste-to-energy projects often adopt DBOT structures to ensure long-term performance efficiency.

Project Suitability Analysis

DBOT models are ideal for complex projects where design quality directly impacts operational efficiency. Single-point responsibility reduces interface risk and improves lifecycle cost control.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Major risks include design errors, technology obsolescence, and long-term O&M performance. These are mitigated through performance-based contracts, availability payments, and lifecycle KPIs.
