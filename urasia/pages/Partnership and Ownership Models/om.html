OM / O&M

Operation & Maintenance

Model Definition

O&M (Operation & Maintenance) is a model where a private or specialized entity is contracted to operate and maintain an asset without involvement in construction, financing, or ownership.

Ownership & Control

  • Ownership remains with public or original private entity
  • Operational control delegated to contractor
  • No construction or capital investment obligation

Strategic Applications

Power Plants & Utilities

Contractors manage day-to-day operations, optimize efficiency, and ensure maintenance standards in existing assets.

Transport & Infrastructure

Roads, airports, railways, and ports often use O&M contracts to enhance service quality and minimize lifecycle costs without asset ownership transfer.

Project Suitability Analysis

O&M is ideal for mature assets where private expertise improves operational efficiency, asset availability, and maintenance quality, without large capital investment.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks include contractor performance, service interruptions, and maintenance cost overruns. Mitigation relies on performance-based contracts, KPIs, and monitoring.
