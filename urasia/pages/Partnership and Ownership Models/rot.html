ROT

Rehabilitate · Operate · Transfer

Model Definition

ROT (Rehabilitate, Operate, Transfer) is a public–private partnership model in which an existing asset is rehabilitated or upgraded by a private entity, operated for a defined concession period, and then transferred back to the public sector.

Ownership & Control

  • Public ownership throughout project life
  • Private operational control during concession
  • Mandatory handback after operation period

Strategic Applications

Roads, Bridges & Tunnels

ROT is widely applied to aging highways, bridges, and tunnels requiring structural upgrades and efficiency improvements without full reconstruction.

Ports, Rail & Energy Assets

Existing ports, railway lines, and power facilities benefit from ROT structures to extend asset life and enhance service quality.

Project Suitability Analysis

ROT models are best suited for mature assets with stable demand but deteriorating performance. They enable rapid upgrades with lower capital exposure compared to greenfield models.

Risk Considerations & Scenarios

Key risks include unknown asset conditions, rehabilitation cost overruns, and operational disruptions. Mitigation relies on thorough technical due diligence, phased rehabilitation, and performance-based contracts.
